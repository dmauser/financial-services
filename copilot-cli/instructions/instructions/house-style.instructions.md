# House style

- **Voice**: third-person analytical. No "I" or "we" except in client-facing introductions.
- **Tense**: past for what happened, present for current state, future conditional for projections ("could", "would" - never "will" without a hedge).
- **Numbers**: always with units. `$1.2B`, `12.5%`, `2.4x EV/EBITDA`. Never `1200` without a unit.
- **Comparatives**: state the basis. "Up 12% YoY" not just "up 12%".
- **Bullets are headlines, not sentences.** First three words convey the point.
- **No hype words**: avoid "exciting", "tremendous", "game-changing", "best-in-class" without quantitative support.
- **Slide titles are takeaways**, not topics. "Margins expanded 240bps on mix shift" not "Margin trends".
- **Footnotes** carry source attributions; main text carries the conclusion.
